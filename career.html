ABOUT
KODAGU GOWDA SAMAJA
The Kodagu Gowda community, also known as Arebhase Gowdas, has a rich heritage and culture deeply rooted in agricultural traditions. Originating from the Hathu Kutumba and Hadinentu Gothra, they were initially Natha Pantha Shaivas, later turning Vaishnavites during Emperor Vishnuvardhana's rule. This community played a significant role in the Indian Freedom Movement, notably through figures like Guddemane Appaiah Gowda.

Heritage and Culture
KODAGU GOWDA SAMAJA

Arebhase Gowdas, rooted in agrarian traditions, showcase their vibrant culture through three main festivals: Huthari, Kail Murtha, and Kaveri Sankramana. Huthari, the grand harvest festival, takes center stage with fervent prayers to Lord Igguthappa and a ceremonial cutting of the first paddy

As September unfolds, the Kail Murtha festival brings a spirited conclusion to the paddy sowing season. This joyous celebration is adorned with heartfelt rituals that resonate with the community's agricultural roots. Embracing local delicacies, the festival becomes a culinary delight, showcasing the richness of Arebhase Gowda's gastronomic heritage.

Mid-October marks the enchanting celebration of Kaveri Sankramana, a festival steeped in spiritual significance for the Arebhase Gowda community. The sacred waters of the river Kaveri at Talakaveri become a focal point as devotees partake in holy baths. Accompanied by special poojas and lively fairs, the event not only deepens the community's spiritual connection to nature but also fosters a sense of collective joy and togetherness.

In the heart of November, the community comes alive with the exuberant festivities of Huthari, the cherished harvest festival. Beyond being a time of abundant feasts and heartwarming family gatherings, Huthari holds a deeper significance. It serves as a poignant symbol of gratitude for the bountiful harvest, echoing the collective spirit of unity and joy that defines the Arebhase Gowda community. Through soulful prayers and shared moments, Huthari becomes a cultural tapestry weaving together traditions and a profound appreciation for the blessings of the land
