ABOUT
KODAGU GOWDA SAMAJA
The Kodagu Gowda community, also known as Arebhase Gowdas, has a rich heritage and culture deeply rooted in agricultural traditions. Originating from the Hathu Kutumba and Hadinentu Gothra, they were initially Natha Pantha Shaivas, later turning Vaishnavites during Emperor Vishnuvardhana's rule. This community played a significant role in the Indian Freedom Movement, notably through figures like Guddemane Appaiah Gowda.

Kodagu Gowda Samaja® Bangalore traces its roots back to a group of community members who migrated from Kodagu to Bangalore in pursuit of employment opportunities. Many found work in various government departments, private organizations, and even established their own successful ventures, contributing to the local Bangalore community.

Inception and Evolution:

In February 1965, under the guidance of visionaries such as Sri. Kedambadi P Mudappa, Sri. Thotambailu Belliappa, and others, the first meeting took place at Krishna Bhavan Restaurant, Malleshwaram. In 1966, Sri. Kedambadi P Mudappa became the inaugural president, marking the beginning of the Kodagu Gowda Samaja® Bangalore's journey. The community's dedication and collaboration laid the foundation for a community base, eventually leading to the establishment of their Samaja in Kaval Byrasandra, RT Nagar.

Infrastructure Development:

Key figures like Sri. Padichettira Mandanna played pivotal roles, acquiring the Kaval Byrasandra, RT Nagar Revenue site for the Samaja. Unfortunately, Mandanna's demise during construction didn't deter progress, and Sri. Thotambailu Belliappa took charge. The community hub flourished, facilitating frequent gatherings, cultural events, and support for Kodagu Arebhase Gowda students pursuing higher education in Bangalore.

Ullala Facility: A New Chapter:

As the community grew, leaders envisioned a larger facility. Approaching the Bangalore Development Authority (BDA) and with the support of Sri. Kombarana G Bopaiah, a significant plot in Vishweshwaraiah Layout, Ullala, measuring nearly 1 acre, was allotted. The foundation laying ceremony on February 23, 2013, marked a significant milestone, attended by notable dignitaries

50th Year Golden Jubilee Celebration:

Under the presidentship of Sri. Amme Sitaram, the Samaja celebrated its 50th Year Golden Jubilee Function on November 3, 2019, at the Ullala Facility. Distinguished guests, including Sri Sri Sri Dr. Nirmalanandanatha Mahaswamiji and Sri. D.V Sadananda Gowda, graced the occasion.

Current Facilities and Leadership:

The Ullala facility houses two community halls, guest rooms, a beautiful lawn, parking, a kitchen, and a dining hall. Sri. Kekada Nanaiah, Retd. DySP, serves as the current President, actively overseeing the completion of the new facility and organizing community-related programs.

Community Engagement:

All community festivals, family gatherings, and events take place at the Samaja. Additionally, the Samaja offers community halls at affordable rates for engagements, marriages, beegara oota, thithi functions, birthdays, and family/community get-togethers.

Acknowledgments:

The Samaja expresses heartfelt gratitude to donors, including Sri. Kekada Nanda, Sri. Kedambadi Mudappa, Sri. Bittera Prabakar, Dr. Bytadka Bojamma, and Sri. Cheriamane Ratnakumar, for their generous contributions.

Festivals Celebrated at Kodagu Gowda Samaja Bangalore

At Kodagu Gowda Samaja® Bangalore, Huttari, Aati and Kailmurtha is celebrated with great Pomp and show. Special Dignitaries and Guests are invited to the samaja at our Ullala, Bangalore Facility and community achievers are honoured. Solo/Group Dance Programs, Singing competitions and Cultural dances are conducted. This is followed by a sumptuous meal and followed by Valga Dance ( Coorg famous music/dance ).

Vidya Yojane

Team KGSB lead by Mr. Thumthajira Sandeep, Govindamana Praveen provide career counselling and help & guide our Arebhase Community Members in their professional and educational front. Several of the community members have got jobs through the Career Counselling group. The Team has also counselled 10th Std. and 12th Std. Students from Cauvery Junior College, Bagamandala and Aruna High School, Cherambane. Further it has also provided monetary help to needy students towards their education fees.
